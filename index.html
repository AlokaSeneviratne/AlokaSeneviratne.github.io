About

I like working close to the hardware. My background started in engineering physics and grew into embedded firmware and IoT system design, along with the sensor and signal work that sits between the electronics and the software. I work across the stack, from low-level firmware and wireless sensor nodes up to the data analysis and machine learning that makes their output useful.

Most of my work now is in Python, with the C and C++ from my embedded background still in regular use, and MATLAB when a problem calls for it.

I also spent time teaching physics, which is where I got into the habit of explaining technical things plainly.
